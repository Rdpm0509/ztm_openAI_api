Spicy Egg and Veggie Wrap

Calories: 400
Servings: 1
  1. Heat 1 teaspoon of olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add 1/2 cup of mixed fresh vegetables (such as bell peppers, onions, and spinach) and sauté until tender.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat 2 eggs and season with salt, pepper, and your favorite spices (such as paprika or cayenne).
  4. Pour the beaten eggs into the skillet with the veggies and scramble until cooked through.
  5. Warm a whole-grain wrap in a dry skillet or microwave for a few seconds.
  6. Place the egg and veggie mixture onto the wrap and roll it up tightly.

Spicy Chickpea Salad

Calories: 350
Servings: 2
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine 1 can of rinsed and drained chickpeas, 1 cup of diced fresh vegetables (such as cucumbers, tomatoes, and red onions), and 1/4 cup of chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley or cilantro).
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together 2 tablespoons of olive oil, the juice of 1 lemon, 1 clove of minced garlic, and your favorite spices (such as cumin or chili powder) to make the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the chickpea mixture and toss to combine.
  4. Adjust the seasoning to taste and add a pinch of salt if desired.
  5. Serve the salad chilled and garnish with a sprinkle of nuts or seeds for extra crunch.

Spicy Baked Salmon with Quinoa

Calories: 500
Servings: 2
  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Rub 2 salmon fillets with a mixture of 1 tablespoon of olive oil, 1 teaspoon of paprika, 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper, and salt to taste.
  3. Place the salmon fillets on the prepared baking sheet and bake for about 12-15 minutes, or until cooked through.
  4. In the meantime, cook 1 cup of quinoa according to package instructions.
  5. Once the quinoa is cooked, fluff it with a fork and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve the spicy baked salmon over the cooked quinoa and garnish with fresh herbs or a squeeze of lemon juice.
