Breakfast:

Title: Mediterranean Omelette with Whole Grain Toast

Ingredients:
- 2 large eggs
- 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
- ¼ cup diced red bell peppers
- ¼ cup diced red onions
- ¼ cup sliced black olives
- ¼ cup chopped fresh spinach
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 slices of whole grain bread

Instructions:
1. In a bowl, whisk the eggs until well beaten.
2. Heat olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
3. Add the bell peppers and onions to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.
4. Add the olives and spinach to the skillet and cook for another 1-2 minutes until spinach wilts.
5. Pour the beaten eggs into the skillet and season with salt and pepper.
6. Cook the omelette for 3-4 minutes until the eggs are set.
7. Meanwhile, toast the whole grain bread slices.
8. Carefully fold the omelette in half and transfer to a plate.
9. Serve the omelette with the toasted whole grain bread.

Total calories: 360 calories
Number of servings: 1

Lunch:

Title: Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

Ingredients:
- 1 cup cooked chickpeas
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 cucumber, diced
- ½ red onion, thinly sliced
- ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
1. In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and parsley.
2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
3. Pour the dressing over the chickpea salad and toss to coat well.
4. Let the salad marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Total calories: 370 calories
Number of servings: 2

Dinner:

Title: Grilled Salmon with Quinoa and Roasted Vegetables

Ingredients:
- 1 salmon fillet (4-6 oz)
- 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ cup cooked quinoa
- 1 cup mixed roasted vegetables (such as zucchini, bell peppers, and carrots)
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
- Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
2. Brush the salmon fillet with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
3. Grill the salmon for 4-5 minutes per side until cooked through.
4. In a separate bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted vegetables, and chopped fresh dill. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
5. Serve the grilled salmon on top of the quinoa and roasted vegetable mixture.

Total calories: 430 calories
Number of servings: 1
